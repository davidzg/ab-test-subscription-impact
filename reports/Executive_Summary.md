# 💰 Gold Subscription A/B Test: Executive Summary
**Status:** Strategic Review Required

**Objective:** Evaluate if the new "Gold Subscription" (gold_sub) increases total revenue or merely cannibalizes existing shop sales.

### The Problem
While the Gold Subscription is popular, our A/B test reveals it is currently acting as a direct substitute rather than an additive revenue stream.

- **High Cannibalization (20.8% Drop):** The introduction of the subscription caused a statistically significant 20.8% decrease in revenue from our "standard" shop items.

- **Neutral Total Impact:** When looking at the "Big Picture" (Subscription + Standard Items), there was no significant increase in total revenue. The p-value (0.72) confirms that the slight 7% variation we saw is just random noise.

- **Revenue Shifting:** Players aren't spending more money; they are simply taking the money they used to spend on individual packs and moving it into the subscription.

### The Insight
The Gold Subscription is a "safe" addition—it doesn't hurt our bottom line—but in its current form, it is not a growth engine. It offers users a different way to pay for what they were already buying without expanding our "paying user" base or increasing the "average spend per user."

### Strategic Recommendations
To turn this into a growth driver, we should pivot the subscription's value proposition:

- **Differentiate the Offering:** Ensure the subscription provides unique perks (e.g., exclusive skins or "Quality of Life" features) that don't overlap with our top-selling one-time packs.

- **Target Non-Spenders:** Position the subscription as a "low-entry" price point specifically for players who haven't made a purchase in 30+ days.

- **Monitor Long-Term Retention:** While the immediate revenue is neutral, subscriptions often improve Long-Term Retention (LTV). We should track these users for another 30–60 days to see if they stay in the game longer than one-time buyers.

### Business Impact
If we leave the subscription as-is, we expect revenue to remain flat. If we implement the "differentiation" strategy, we can protect our existing shop revenue while using the subscription to capture new spending.
